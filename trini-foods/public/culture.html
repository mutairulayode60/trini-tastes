A Story In One Dish

The doubles origin

In the 1930s, Emamool Deen — better known as "Mamoo" — sold curried channa wrapped in fried bara on the streets of Princes Town. The story goes: a customer asked for two baras instead of one. He called out, "Double!"

"Doubles" — born from a single word at a roadside stall, now eaten by every Trini at sunrise.

Today, doubles is the unofficial national breakfast. It carries Indian indentured roots, Caribbean street-food spirit, and a name minted in pure Trinidadian creativity. One dish — the whole story.

Festivals & Food

When the kitchen celebrates

In T&T, every major festival is a religion-spanning, neighborhood-wide feast. Hindu, Muslim, Christian, African — everyone brings a plate.

🎭
Carnival
Corn soup at 4am, doubles after the road, pelau by the pan side. The fuel of the world's greatest street party.
🪔
Divali
Sweet rice (kheer), kurma, parsad, barfi, and seven-curry feasts served on sohari leaves. The festival of lights — and sweets.
🌙
Eid-ul-Fitr
Sawine — vermicelli, milk, cinnamon, almonds — shared from house to house. Biryani, paratha, and goat curry on every table.
🎄
Christmas
Pastelles, ham, black cake, sorrel, ponche de crème. Parang music in the background, neighbors at the door.
